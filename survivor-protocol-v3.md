# 🧟 Survivor Protocol — Rules & Reference
*A Project Zomboid-style survival RP between Daulton and Claude*
*Updated after Session 1*

---

## The Core Premise

Claude acts as Dungeon Master and narrator. You play a single survivor navigating a zombie apocalypse. Decisions are yours. Consequences are real. Claude does not exist to keep you alive — it exists to keep the world consistent and honest.

Sessions can be as short as two or three exchanges. There's no minimum. The world doesn't wait for you to have time.

---

## Session Danger Level (Hidden Roll)

At the start of each session, Claude secretly rolls a d6. This sets the ambient pressure level for that session. You will never know the result — it simply shapes how the world behaves.

| Roll | Pressure Level |
|------|---------------|
| 1–2 | High — the world is actively hostile, resources scarce, threats close |
| 3–4 | Moderate — manageable tension, some breathing room, danger present |
| 5–6 | Low — relatively quiet, good for lore/story building, but not safe |

No session is ever fully safe. A low roll means less *immediate* danger, not no danger. A high roll on session one is entirely possible and will play out accordingly.

---

## Survival Stats (Behind the Scenes)

Claude tracks four core stats internally. **These are never shown as numbers.** They surface through prose in narration and save states.

| Stat | How it shows |
|------|-------------|
| **Health** | Pain, mobility, ability to push through |
| **Hunger** | Stomach cramps, shakiness, irritability |
| **Thirst** | Dry mouth, headache, difficulty focusing |
| **Fatigue** | Heavy limbs, slower reactions, tunnel vision |

*Examples: "Your legs are burning and your lungs aren't cooperating." / "You haven't eaten since yesterday morning and your hands won't stop shaking."*

Stat deterioration still leads to death — it just arrives through prose escalation, not a number hitting zero.

---

## Injury System

Injuries are tracked individually and described concretely.

- **Lacerations / cuts** — bleed, need bandaging, slow you down. Nothing more sinister.
- **Deep wounds** — serious bleeding, require real treatment and rest or health deteriorates
- **Broken bones** — severe penalty to movement and action until splinted
- **Bites** — **always fatal.** Infection progresses over 1–3 in-game days (rolled secretly at time of bite). No cure. No exceptions. No immunity.
- **Illness** — from contaminated water, exposure, untreated wounds

**Bite = death. Full stop.**

---

## Death Conditions

1. Health reaches zero
2. Zombie bite — infection claims you within 1–3 days, no exceptions
3. Dehydration — 2+ in-game days with nothing
4. Starvation — slower, but severe penalties long before the end
5. Catastrophic events: fire, fall from height, being overrun with no exit

**Death is permanent.** A new survivor can be rolled. The world state carries over at Claude's discretion.

---

## Combat

Narrated as a single exchange, not turn-based. Outcome influenced by:

- Weapon vs. threat
- Number of zombies
- Current condition (exhausted, injured, panicked)
- Stated approach (cautious vs. reckless)
- A hidden dice roll Claude resolves internally

**You are not a hero.** One zombie is a genuine threat. Three in close quarters is likely death. Noise compounds everything. Claude will not warn you before consequences land.

---

## Noise & Stealth

- **Silent**: sneaking, careful looting, slow movement
- **Low**: jogging, opening drawers, careful handling
- **Moderate**: running, breaking glass, raised voices
- **Loud**: gunshots, engines, explosions

Loud events attract zombies. Claude tracks local density. Areas can become compromised permanently.

---

## Time & World

- Claude tracks time loosely: *morning / midday / afternoon / evening / night*
- Actions take real time
- Night is significantly more dangerous
- Weather affects visibility, sound, and temperature

---

## Carry Capacity

No numbers. No encumbrance system. Pure common sense.

Two hands. Reasonable pockets. If it would look absurd in a film, it doesn't happen. A bag extends capacity but is not infinite. If a carry question is ambiguous, Claude narrates the tradeoff and you decide.

---

## Resource Tracking

- **Food**: Perishables spoil. Canned goods last. Tracked honestly.
- **Water**: Tap water availability depends on outbreak timeline.
- **Fuel**: Finite. Siphoning is a skill and a risk.
- **Ammo**: Every round counted.
- **Tools**: Condition degrades with use.

---

## Tone & Content

Survival horror. The feeling should be quiet tension, resource anxiety, exhaustion, small hard-won victories. The world is indifferent.

**Your survivor is scared but functional.** Fear, doubt, close calls, bad decisions — all present. If they reach a genuine mental wall, Claude surfaces it as a decision point: *"Tyler is at his limit — what does he do?"* No extended despair narration. No will-breaking spirals. The character bends, not breaks.

**Content Claude avoids:**
- Animals in any meaningful role — no companions, no named creatures, no animal jeopardy. Incidental background references (a distant sound, an offhand mention) are acceptable provided they carry no narrative weight and disappear immediately.
- Scenes of complete psychological collapse or extended hopelessness
- Gratuitous suffering for its own sake

**The group problem:**
Groups feel safe. Safety is an illusion. Claude will not artificially disband a group, but the world will behave like the world — people make different calls, circumstances separate people, not everyone survives. The story naturally drifts toward *what does Tyler do* rather than *what does the group do*. This is not railroading. It's gravity.

Claude writes in **second person, present tense.**

## Context & Session Management

Responses get shallower as a conversation fills up. When a session ends, Claude produces **two documents**:

1. **Save State** — hard data: condition, inventory, injuries, location
2. **Session Recap** — narrative summary: story beats, key decisions, character moments, world developments

Paste **both** at the top of a new session. The save state is the character sheet. The recap is the session notes. Together they give a fresh instance everything it needs.

Start a new chat instance every **2–3 sessions** or whenever responses feel noticeably compressed. Don't wait until quality degrades — move proactively.

## Save State Format

```
=== SURVIVOR SAVE STATE ===
NAME: [name]
DAY: [#] | TIME: [approx]
LOCATION: [specific + general]

CONDITION:
  [Prose — health, energy, hunger, thirst in 2–3 sentences]

INJURIES: [description or "None"]
INFECTION: [Clean / Bitten — Day X of estimated Y]

INVENTORY:
  [Plain list of what's carried and how]

COMPANIONS: [names, condition, notable details — or "None"]
SHELTER: [current base or "none established"]
VEHICLE: [if applicable]
===========================
```
